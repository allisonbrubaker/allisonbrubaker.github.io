Printed Couture: 3D Printed Dress
code-made lace • 3D print

Printed Couture: 3D Printed Dress

This final project for my Session A Media Arts & Design class was a collaborative exploration of how fashion, coding, and digital fabrication can come together to push the boundaries of creative expression. Working with a partner, we designed a custom lace system using a code-based approach grounded in repetition, transformation, and recursion. Our goal was to generate intricate lace-like patterns that could be 3D printed and sewn directly into a dress, blending the delicate aesthetic of traditional lace with the sharp innovation of computational design.

Adobe Illustrator — Apparel
vector art • apparel

Adobe Illustrator — Apparel

This project focuses on a piece I designed in Adobe Illustrator. These designs are all built from scratch using the pen tool, custom gradients, and hand-colored swatches. I focused on layering each element intentionally to bring depth, dimension, and a more lifelike quality to otherwise flat vector forms. One of the standout pieces from this series is a custom floral pattern I designed entirely in Illustrator. After experimenting with swatches and redrawing each flower and connecting line digitally, I printed the pattern onto a hoodie to turn the illustration into wearable art. Overall, this project pushed me to refine my digital art techniques while also exploring how my designs could live beyond the screen.

Hanging Installation
ribbon • balance • space

Hanging Installation

This project is a hanging installation I designed and built for my home using circular metal rings and individually tied ribbon. Each piece was assembled by hand, layering ribbon around the ring and then suspending the structure from the ceiling using string. One of the biggest challenges was achieving balance, both physically and visually. This installation reflects my interest in texture, symmetry, and spatial design.

Doily Skirt
vintage crochet • wearable

Doily Skirt

For this project, I sewed together a collection of vintage doilies to create a wearable piece that works as a belt or beach cover-up. I braided yarn to attach the piece, giving it a cohesive, handmade feel while showcasing the delicate patterns of the fabric. Doilies, once symbols of domestic skill and class in the 17th-century Victorian era, are often overlooked today despite their intricate craftsmanship. I wanted to give these forgotten objects new life through fashion, moving them from simple decoration into something wearable. This experiment inspired me to consider expanding the idea into a full doily set, as I continue exploring the history and artistry behind this craft.

Laser-Cut Roxy Coaster
code → coaster

Laser-Cut Roxy Coaster

For this project, I combined coding and digital fabrication to create a custom Roxy logo coaster. I first used Processing to generate the initial layout and dot pattern, then imported the design into Adobe Illustrator to refine the vector elements for precision. Once finalized, I prepared the file for laser cutting using both cutting and engraving techniques to bring the design to life on a physical material. This project was a hands-on exploration of how code-based design can translate into tactile, functional objects. It reflects the intersection of branding, form, and fabrication, and was a great way to merge aesthetic choices with technical workflow.

Tea Bag Organizer
press-fit • storage

Tea Bag Organizer

For this project, I designed and fabricated a press-fit tea bag organizer to solve a real problem in my tiny kitchen. I started by sketching and measuring my tea bags, then created a parameterized model in Fusion 360. After preparing the file in Illustrator, I laser cut the pieces, which required some troubleshooting and adjustments when a few joints didn’t fit properly. With some sanding and refinements, I was able to assemble the organizer and learned a lot about precision and constraints along the way. The finished piece now hangs by my microwave and keeps my tea collection neat and accessible, with ideas for future engraving or decoration.

Doily Set (WIP)
heritage × fashion

Doily Set (WIP)

Inspired by my earlier doily skirt, I wanted to expand the idea into a full set while focusing more on the cultural significance of doilies. For centuries, these intricate handmade pieces represented domestic skill and artistry, yet they are often overlooked in the present. Through this project, my goal is to give credit to the past while reintroducing doilies into modern fashion. By blending their historical symbolism with contemporary design, I hope to highlight their beauty and bring this craft into a new context. This work-in-progress is both a fashion experiment and a way of honoring the artistry behind a forgotten practice.

Laser + Denim (WIP)
laser-cut jeans

Laser + Denim (WIP)

For this work-in-progress, I wanted to experiment with laser cutting on clothing materials like denim by reworking a pair of jeans. My process involves creating and designing a pattern, laser cutting it directly into the fabric, and then sewing the altered pieces together. This project is inspired by Iris van Herpen’s laser-cut couture line, which combines fashion with digital design and fabrication. By applying similar techniques to everyday materials like denim, I am exploring how digital fabrication can transform ordinary garments into unique, statement-making pieces. The project is still developing, but it has already pushed me to think about how technology and fashion can intersect in accessible ways.

Haleakalā Poster
Illustrator poster

Haleakalā Poster

This project features a poster I created in Adobe Illustrator, all built from scratch using the pen tool, custom gradients, and hand-colored swatches. I focused on layering each element intentionally to bring depth, dimension, and a more lifelike quality to otherwise flat vector forms. The piece began as a blank canvas, and I let shape, color, and careful composition guide the design process. The image in this series took me nearly a full month to complete due to the incredibly detailed process of editing and manually filling in hundreds of small, intricate spaces. I was inspired by a picture I took and used that as inspiration to try to create a national park poster based on the image I took. This project especially taught me the value of patience and how digital tools can be used in a painterly, expressive way.
